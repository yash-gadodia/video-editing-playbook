# Case study: @gabrieljudah_ AI-demo reel (122s, talking-head + screen-recording format)

Frame-by-frame study (2fps contact sheets + scene-cut detection + full transcript) of a high-engagement AI-tool demo reel. This format is the closest analogue to our tech/BTS reels (founder explains a system, screen recordings as proof).

## The numbers
- 122s runtime. 20 hard scene cuts, but *perceived* pace is much faster: captions change every 1.5-2s, overlay animations run continuously, and the presenter's location/framing resets mid-monologue.
- Cut density is not uniform: it CLUSTERS. 8 cuts in 6 seconds during the "receipts" montage (44-50s), sparse during explanation beats. Pacing = rhythm, not constant speed.

## Script structure (the retention skeleton)
| Time | Beat | Line pattern |
|---|---|---|
| 0-6s | Value promise + negative triple | "You can now generate ad creatives for free... no editor, no videographer, and no CMO needed" |
| 6-13s | Free offer, zero risk | "everything I'm about to show you, you can do it for free" |
| 13-18s | Scarcity twist | "But here's the catch... going away very soon" |
| 18-25s | Stakes via other people's mistake | "Most people are about to make a huge mistake... left with nothing" |
| 25-37s | Promise of the opposite | "I'm going to show you how to do the opposite" |
| 37-49s | Credibility receipt | "That video has gone viral" (shows the view counter on screen) |
| 49-90s | Steps 1-2-3 | numbered sticker badges; step 3 flagged as "the part that really matters" |
| 90-115s | Aphorism payoff | "the intelligence isn't in the model anymore, it is in the skill" |
| 115-122s | CTA | comment-keyword ("Comment FABLE 5") |

Reusable skeleton: **promise → free/easy → catch → most-people-mistake → opposite → receipts → steps → quotable line → comment-keyword CTA.**

## Editing techniques observed (transferable)
1. **Pinned context banner** top-center for the first ~10s ("GENERATE VIDEOS WITH FABLE 5 USING HIGGSFIELD FOR FREE!") - tells scrollers what the video is even mid-scroll. Out of notch zone.
2. **Caption pops**: 2-5 words max, bold white + drop shadow, center-frame at ~65-70% height, replaced on nearly every phrase. Never full sentences.
3. **Talking head ↔ screen recording alternation** every 2-4s. Face returns for emphasis beats ("Step 3, and this is the part that really matters" = always said to camera).
4. **Presenter never leaves**: during screen segments he stays as a cutout/PiP at bottom, mic in hand. Continuity of character.
5. **Live progress-bar overlay as retention device**: a "+100 CREDITS ADDED" gamified bar animates from 38→81→100 across several seconds WHILE he talks. Gives the eye a moving target during a static talking-head beat.
6. **Location/framing resets mid-monologue**: same VO sentence continues but the backdrop/angle changes (bedroom → screen room → couch). Cheap way to add "cuts" without cutting the audio. Includes deliberate motion-blur whip movements between lines.
7. **Numbered step stickers**: "02 CONNECT CLAUDE TO HIGGSFIELD MCP 🔗" white sticker + colored number badge, slightly rotated, persists through the whole step. Viewers always know where they are.
8. **Raw > sterile screen proof**: screen "recordings" are often a phone filming a glowing monitor at a slight angle - reads as receipts, not as a produced ad. Intercut with clean captures.
9. **Receipts montage**: real results (view counts, screenshots with hand-drawn arrow stickers, "146k/583k views" overlays) machine-gunned in the fastest-cut section of the video.
10. **Visual matching of lists**: when VO says "2nd draft, 3rd draft, 4th draft", the screen shows draft 2, 3, 4. Every enumerated word gets its own visual.

## What we adopt for The Bon Pet
- The script skeleton above for tech/system reels (OMS, automation, "how we run a pet food brand with AI").
- Caption pops (already in Remotion pipeline) but SHORTER: 2-4 words, not sentence fragments.
- Cut clustering: bunch the fastest cuts around the receipts beat, slow down for the single key explanation.
- A live counter/progress overlay during talking-head beats (orders count, kg cooked, reviews ticking up).
- Numbered step stickers for any how-to reel.
- Face returns to camera for the ONE line that matters most.
