# Case study: @kaiandjia (SG couple, cake business) — how a 6K-follower account gets 5.8M-view reels

Downloaded + frame-studied their top 5 reels by views (5.8M, 4.6M, 690K, 341K, 74K). SG-local, founder-led, small account — the closest structural analogue to The Bon Pet (small SG brand, founder faces, product embedded in story).

## The headline lesson
Their two megahits (5.8M + 4.6M) have **zero voiceover and near-zero editing**. The virality is 100% premise + emotional stakes. Their *product* reels with fast editing top out at ~50-700K. **Editing amplifies a good premise; it cannot rescue a weak one.**

## Reel-by-reel anatomy

### 1. "Rock bottom → 7-figure cake shop" (5.8M views, 68s)
- Format: **caption-driven photo-dump story**. Real archival photos/clips from 2018→now, one 2-line caption per shot, music bed only, NO voiceover.
- Pacing: 55 cuts in 68s = **1.24s average shot**. Photo-dump rhythm.
- Arc: breakup → clubbing/lost purpose → Tinder → "just a one-time thing" → fell in love → proved myself → married her → "we grew her family's humble cake shop into a 7-figure business" → cakes for celebrities.
- **The business payoff is smuggled inside a love story.** You watch for the romance; the brand rides along and the flex lands at the end where completion is highest.
- Every caption line is matched to a REAL photo from that exact life era. Authenticity is the production value.

### 2. "I married the guy that cheated on me" (4.6M views, 13.5s)
- Format: ONE continuous selfie-walk clip of the happy couple. A single static caption pinned the entire video: "I married the guy that cheated on me, but I am not afraid to admit that:" — ends with a COLON.
- The colon is an **open loop resolved only in the post caption** (a numbered list). Forces caption-read → dwell time → algorithm gold. Comments explode with opinions = more fuel.
- Trending audio with ironic lyric pairing ("I think of you all the time / now that you're gone" under a thriving marriage).
- **Total edit effort: ~zero.** The provocative-confession hook does everything.

### 3. "Baking a cake for our homie Tony" (690K, 32s)
- Format: fast VO-narrated client-cake build. 26 cuts in 32s = **1.23s/shot**.
- B2B/client work reframed as a friendship story ("our homie"), not a portfolio piece.
- Dialogue beats between the couple ("He's a legend in the industry" / "Yeah, for sure") break up the narration.

### 4. "Mr Koh" grandfather reel (341K, 55s) — family character story, wholesome angle, real person the audience roots for.

## The kaiandjia formula (transferable to The Bon Pet)
1. **Premise > polish.** Budget your effort: 80% on the hook/premise, 20% on the edit. A confession, a stakes-reveal, or a transformation arc beats any transition pack.
2. **Personal arc as trojan horse for the brand.** "We built this" lands harder than "we sell this". Bon Pet version: the founder story (why two SWEs started cooking pet food), the kitchen pivot, the first 548 customers — with the product as the payoff, not the topic.
3. **Caption-driven photo-dump** is a legitimate megahit format and the CHEAPEST to produce: real old photos + 2-line captions + music. No VO, no filming day. We have 2 years of real archive (NAS) for this.
4. **Open-loop pinned caption ending in ":"** that resolves in the post caption = dwell-time hack. Use sparingly, needs a genuinely spicy confession ("We lost $600 on our own order:" etc).
5. **Emotional stakes taxonomy that works for a small brand:** vulnerability (rock bottom), loyalty (homie/family), pride-in-craft (perfection under deadline), wholesome real people (Mr Koh / our customers' pets).
6. **When VO-driven, cut every ~1.2-1.4s.** Their product reels never sit on a shot longer than 2s.
7. **Ironic/emotional trending-audio pairing** on no-VO reels - the lyric does the emotional captioning.
