# The Viral Content Formula

Synthesized from frame-by-frame study of 9 reference reels (pet-brand founder series, food ASMR, narrator-trend explainers, a vet-nutritionist myth-bust, a B2B animated explainer, and two pet-comedy formats), plus our own two best-performing cuts. This is the recipe for engaging, shareable short-form brand content.

## 0. The one law
**Watch time + completion + shares are the only signals that matter.** Every rule below serves those. Cut to the story's natural length, then kill every dead second.

## 1. Story = premise → conflict → resolution (lead with the CONFLICT)
Every viral reel is a tiny story:
- **Conflict/HOOK first** (0-2s): the pattern-interrupt. A bold claim ("your beef kibble is barely beef"), a question, a relatable pain ("dog owners, I need your help!"), or curiosity ("this combo never misses").
- **Setup/stakes** (2-10s): why it matters to *you*.
- **Evidence/receipts** (10-25s): real proof - contrast, a real label, a stat, product macros.
- **Payoff/resolution** (25-38s): the answer / the product, shown not sold.
- **End card**: follow CTA or branded card. Never a hard sell.

**One idea per video. 25-45s. If it covers two ideas, split it.**

## 2. The hook (first 2 seconds decide everything)
- **Visual pattern-interrupt** on frame 1: appetizing macro, chaos, or a striking real shot. NEVER open on a logo/static card.
- **Text hook** on screen immediately (bold, short). Title/series badge drops a *beat later*, not at 0s.
- **Series framing** for retention + binge: "ep.1", "ATTN: PAWRENTS", a named recurring segment - a recurring badge/sting brands it as a series people follow.

## 3. Voice & delivery (this is what makes it feel human)
The reference creators all sound **conversational, enthusiastic, and personal** - varied intonation, talking TO one person, imperfect and real. Flat/monotone kills it.
- Talk to "you" like a friend, not an audience. Energy up, pace quick, personality on.
- Emphasis through delivery: lean into the punch words.
- **AI voice clones:** push settings for energy (stability low ~0.10-0.14, style high ~0.66-0.75, speed ~1.08), BUT the true ceiling is the training data - a **hyped human training take is the real unlock**. Write scripts punchy: short sentences, questions, "?!", one idea per line.

## 4. Captions (read-with-sound-off)
- **Bottom band, sentence-case, synced phrase-by-phrase** to the voice (all 9 refs do this).
- **Keyword POPS**: blow up the punch noun, accent color, spring-in.
- Safe zones: never in the top ~18% of frame (notch + IG UI covers it).

## 5. Visuals & editing
- **Real footage first, text = accent** (never walls of text on a plain bg). Phone-shot, natural grade > cinematic.
- **Fast cuts** ~1.3-2.5s/shot; one-clip-per-VO-line montage (the best food reference: ~47 cuts in 68s). Kill dead air with jump-cuts.
- **Product/food MACROS** = appetizing ASMR (scooping, toppings, a spoon drop, a dog demolishing a bowl).
- **A character on screen**: a founder's face or the pet. People follow faces + pets.
- **Contrast** is the killer structure: their label → our real bowl ("theirs vs ours").
- **Everything still is STATIC.** No zoompan (integer-pixel rounding = jitter), no crop-pan drift on stills. Only real video clips move - they have their own motion.

## 6. Animated accents (the polish layer)
Built in Remotion (React-coded video): ingredient/emoji **cutouts pop in**, bold **arched display text**, **draw-on circles** over receipts, **stat count-ups**, clean **gradient end-cards**. Spring physics, never jittery zoompan.

## 7. Engagement levers (make them share/save/comment)
- **Save-worthy**: receipts, feeding info, "how to read a label" - people screenshot it.
- **Shareable**: a contrarian truth they'll send to a fellow pet parent.
- **Comment bait**: end on a question ("what's YOUR dog's food say?").
- **Loop-friendly** ending. **Follow CTA** on the happy payoff, never a sell.

## 7b. Music
A low music bed HELPS (fills silence between VO words → retention, adds pace/energy; every reference reel has one). It only CONFUSES when: (1) it has **lyrics** competing with the VO, (2) it's **too loud** (keep ~8-12%, felt-not-heard, under a loudnorm'd VO), or (3) **tone mismatch** - a comedic/bouncy track undercuts a credibility exposé. For myth-busts use **driving/modern/slightly-edgy instrumental**, not jokey. **BUT for a fact-dense, VO-driven reel, NO music can be better** - the words carry it and any bed competes for comprehension. Default: try it clean first; only add a bed if the VO feels dry. Always also export a **clean no-music version** so the founder can drop a trending sound in-app (best for reach). CC-BY tracks (Kevin MacLeod / Incompetech) need a caption credit; Pixabay/Uppbeat/Mixkit = no-attribution.

## 8. Small details that separate good from viral
- Persistent brand badge (a corner, off the notch) = looks like a series.
- Consistent brand kit (same fonts + palette) every time = recognizable in the feed.
- Emoji per beat. Trending audio low under the VO (added in-app).
- Film everything, always - "people care about stories, not perfect angles."

## The 5-beat template to copy every time
1. **HOOK** (0-3s): bold claim/question + pattern-interrupt real shot + text hook.
2. **STAKES** (3-10s): why you should care.
3. **RECEIPTS** (10-25s): real proof - contrast / real label / stat diagram / macros.
4. **PAYOFF** (25-38s): the real product, shown. The resolution.
5. **END**: follow CTA / branded card. Question for the comments.
